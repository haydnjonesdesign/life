**Ingredients**
- 1 cup pumpkin _chopped into cubes_
- ⅓ cup light flavoured oil _olive, canola or vegetable oil_
- ⅓ cup natural or Greek yoghurt
- 2 eggs
- **3** tbsp milk 
- 1 tsp bicarb soda
- 1 pinch salt
- 1 tsp dried herbs _we used rosemary and thyme_
- ¼ cup grated parmesan cheese
- 1 ½ cups plain flour

**Instructions**
- Preheat the oven to 180°C (160°C fan forced). Grease and line a loaf pan.  
- Steam pumpkin until soft. Roughly mash with a fork and set aside to cool.  
- Sift flour, bicarb and salt into a bowl, and set aside.  
- In a large bowl combine together oil and yoghurt, then whisk in eggs.
- Add the pumpkin, milk, parmesan cheese and herbs to the yoghurt mix and combine.  
- Fold in sifted dry ingredients until just combined. Do not over mix.  
- Pour into the prepared tin and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a skewer comes out clean.  
- Allow to cool before slicing.  
- To make the whipped feta, place ricotta and feta into a food processor and blend until smooth. Place into a serving bowl and drizzle with honey and top with fresh thyme. This isn’t suitable for babies due to the salt content and honey, but is delicious for the rest of the family to enjoy. You can serve your baby’s pumpkin bread plain, or spread with ricotta, natural Greek yoghurt, hommus or leftover puree.
