Under 30 mins

Makes about 9 so double recipe to make a bigger batch

**Ingredients**
- 1 small tin (95g) tuna or salmon _in springwater or oil_
- 1/2 cup vegetables _cooked and mashed (see notes)_
- 1 egg _lightly beaten (or_ [_egg replacement_](https://babymealtimes.com.au/recipe-collection/egg-replacement-binding/)_)_
- 1 tbsp breadcrumbs
- 1 tbsp olive oil _for cooking_ 

**Instructions**
- Drain excess oil or springwater from tinned fish.  
- Combine fish, mashed vegetables, egg and breadcrumbs in a bowl.  
- Heat a non-stick frying pan on medium heat, add a tablespoon of oil and scoop large spoonfuls of mixture into the pan. Flatten slightly with the back of the spoon.  
- Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown.  
- Allow to cool slightly before serving.  

**Recipe Notes**
You can use any mashed vegetables, but this recipe works particularly well with mashed potato, pumpkin and sweet potato. 

If you don’t have breadcrumbs, you can use crushed Weet-Bix, oat bran or plain flour instead. 

The fish cakes may be delicate while hot, but they become firmer as they cool.
