Under 30 mins

This is another favourite recipe across all of the Mealtimes programs. To mix things up, you can also add tinned tuna, fresh herbs, different grated vegetables, or peas and corn. You could also shape these into patties and serve as a vegetarian burger.

This recipe contains cheese which is a nutritious but salty food. If your baby enjoys these, be mindful of offering other high [salt](https://babymealtimes.com.au/salt-for-babies/) food that day.

**Ingredients**
- 160 g sweet potato _diced_
- 125 g ricotta
- 60 g parmesan _grated_
- 1 egg _or_ [_egg replacement_](https://babymealtimes.com.au/recipe-collection/egg-replacement-binding/)
- 1 ⅓ cup cooked couscous _from ⅔ cup uncooked couscous_

**Instructions**
- Place the sweet potato in a steamer and steam for 10 minutes or until soft enough to mash with a fork.
- Mash the sweet potato, breaking up the skin as you go. Allow to cool slightly.
- Pre-heat oven to 200°C (180°C fan forced).  
- Add ⅔ cup cooked couscous, egg, ricotta and parmesan and mix well to combine.  
- Roll mixture into balls (approx. 1-2 tbsp of mixture per ball).  
- Place the ball into a dish of the remaining cooked couscous and roll until covered.  
- Place on a lined baking tray and bake for 15 minutes.
- Allow to cool or serve warm.
