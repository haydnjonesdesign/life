Under 30 mins

**Ingredients**
- ½ medium zucchini _grated_
- ½ medium carrot _grated_
- ½ cup fresh or tinned corn kernels _approx. ½ corn cob_ 
- 1 tbsp fresh herbs _chopped_ 
- 1 cup cheddar _grated_
- ½ cup plain, natural or Greek yoghurt
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 egg
- 1 cup wholemeal self-raising flour 

**Instructions**
- Preheat oven to 200°C (180°C fan forced)  
- Press grated zucchini and carrot into a sieve to remove as much liquid as possible (if you can be bothered!)  
- Place the zucchini, carrot, corn, herbs, yoghurt, olive oil, egg and half the cheese in a bowl and mix well until combined.  
- Fold flour into the wet mix gently, until just combined.  
- Grease muffin trays (or bake in two batches). Spoon the mixture, equally, between 12 muffin sections (around two heaped tbsp each) and top evenly with the remaining cheese. Bake for 25-30 mins.  
- Cool for 5 minutes in the muffin tray before using a knife to remove to cool on a wire rack.

**Recipe Notes**
We used rosemary and thyme. If using dried herbs, reduce to 1/2 tsp. 
If you make mini muffins, reduce cooking time to 15 mins.
Additions to these muffins could include:
- Finely chopped broccoli
- Chopped English spinach
- 2 tbsp [pesto](https://babymealtimes.com.au/basic-pesto/) 
- ½ cup ricotta
- Crumbled/mashed tofu

Let your muffins cool completely before freezing so when stored they don’t end up soggy. They’ll keep for 3 months in the freezer.

Thaw from freezer to oven at 160°C until warmed through or thaw on the bench then warm in the microwave for a few seconds.

If storing short term, muffins are best kept at room temp in a sealed container.
