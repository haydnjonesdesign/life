Under 30 mins

Scones are easier to make than you might think. This blueberry scone recipe will be a family favourite for years to come.

**Ingredients**
- 2 cups self-raising flour
- 60 g butter _chilled and chopped_
- ½ cup frozen blueberries
- 1-2 tsp lemon zest _optional_
- ¾ cup milk
- 2 tsp lemon juice
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- Extra milk for brushing

**Instructions**
- Preheat the oven to 220°C (200°C fan-forced). Check your oven rack is positioned towards the top half of the oven.
- Sift the flour into a bowl. Using your fingertips, rub butter into the flour until all of the large lumps of butter are gone and the mixture looks like fine breadcrumbs.  
- Stir through the frozen blueberries (and lemon rind if using).
- Mix together milk, lemon juice and vanilla in a jug. Add the milk mixture to the flour mixture. Using the blunt side of a butter knife, cut through the mixture to combine into a sticky dough. Don’t over-mix.
- Turn out sticky dough onto a lightly floured surface. Knead gently until just smooth.
- Use a rolling pin to flatten dough to about 2 cm thick. Using a cookie cutter (approx. 5-6cm diameter), or sharp edged jar/glass/round container, cut out scones. Press leftover dough together. Repeat until all dough is used. Note: Dough will likely get more sticky as blueberries start to defrost, so sprinkle with extra flour as needed.
- Place scones, just touching, on a lined baking tray.
- Lightly brush scones with milk, before baking for 15 minutes or until golden brown.
- Allow scones to cool slightly before using a knife to separate any scones that have baked together. Serve with ricotta, cream cheese, thick cream, butter and/or chia jam.

**Recipe Notes**
Tips for light scones:
- Keep them cool - use cold butter and try not to warm the mix with your hands
- Don’t overmix at any stage - use your fingertips and a light touch
- Work quickly - so the blueberries don’t defrost and the butter stays firm

You can swap blueberries for:
- Sultanas, diced apple and cinnamon
- Diced dates or other dried fruit

**Texture considerations**
This is generally a level C finger food, but texture can vary based on a number of factors. Always check the texture before offering to your baby. Check out the [Finger Food Gallery](https://babymealtimes.com.au/finger-food-gallery/) for more.

**Time savers**
Use a food processor/Thermomix to blitz sifted flour and butter together instead of using fingers.

**Leftovers**
- Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days or freeze for up to 3 months.
- Leftovers can be served at room temperature or warm, for best results wrap in alfoil and reheat in the oven.
